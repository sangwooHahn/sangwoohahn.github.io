UI · UX / Product Design

MICARD

MICARD is a combination of word MICA and Card. It is a mobile unit application service for MICA students. MICARD replaces all the functions of plastic cards based on NFC technology. It helps users enjoy campus life more comfortably.

Current Problem

MICA has many applications for better school life. There are more than six applications, but some are outdated and no longer updated. Another problem is that not all applications are designed by MICA. So students have to download different apps for each function.

Design Goal

MICARD’s design goal is to transfer all the functions of the school ID card to the mobile and integrate existing apps. After organizing all the functions into a list, group them into categories with similar functions. The categories are largely divided into account, dining, and life. MICARD's functions focused on personal life, not on the classes and communication. It only has the functions that users need to use the card or while staying on the school campus.

Persona Scenario

Dormitory Resident

He is a student living in a dormitory. He has to show his ID every time he enters the dormitory. He wants to check the empty washing machine instead of going to the laundry room. He wants to make mobile payments both in laundry and vending machines. And he wants the alarm to be set automatically whenever he do the laundry.

Night Worker

She does her homework in the studio until late at night and uses a shuttle to go back to her home. She also uses the shuttle to go to the H-mart or restaurant. She wants to check the shuttle schedule and request a shuttle via mobile and check real-time location.

Cafe Doris Lover

She often buys and eats food at Cafe Doris. She wants to pre-order food and pick it up right away before lunchtime. If she didn’t order in advance, she can go directly to Doris, order food, and pay via mobile. Sometimes she compares food on her mobile and goes to Meyerhoff Dining Hall.

Resolutions

The functions of the ID card, self-certification, and payment method transfer to the smartphone. The users open the door using a smartphone and request a shuttle. Users can pay for their food before lunchtime and get a notification when order is complete. And users no longer have to go directly to the laundry room to check the empty washing machine. It eliminates the inconvenience of school life beyond the integration of cards.
