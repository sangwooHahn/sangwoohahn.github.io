Game Design / UI · UX Design

Daydream

Daydream is an action game developed in collaboration with a programmer for three months. It tells the story of challengers who enter the tower and fight monsters to find the magical stone Loar that grants their wishes.

Target Audience

Daydream is a game for people who enjoy the games and feel a sense of accomplishment through difficulty. It is set in a medieval Western fantasy and contains its own story and worldview. Games develop with console and PC versions so that users can enjoy the battle with large screens and control the joysticks.

Game Graphics

Character Design

The characters’ design has a condensed appearance to make the movement easier because of the short development period. All motions make with frame-by-frame animations. To preserve the identity of the character, each character is assigned a symbolic color and weapon.

Environment Design

The fixed environment creates in 3D to avoid monotony and gives a sense of space. The camera is fixed in one direction and moves only left and right to evade awkward combinations of 2D and 3D. The area designs to remind the old place of the tower, where the Golem of Knowledge stays.

Game Interface

Interface Research

The game is design for a console and a PC. The game does not use a mouse even in PC because the video games do not have a mouse pointer. All buttons design with dynamic interfaces, designed for the use of various functions with minimal buttons. Before the battle stage, It allows preparation progress mostly with confirmation and cancellation buttons.

Usability Tests

Title & Character Page

· Do you understand how to control it?
· Do you realize you have to choose two characters to proceed?
· Is the icon enough to mark the selected character?

During the Game

· Where is the character's health and how much is it?
· Can you find the key for switching characters?
· Where is the health point of your second character?
· Were is the boss' health status?
· If you forgot control keys, where would you find them?
· Where is the detailed description of the skill?
· Do you know that each skills consumes a different amount of energy?
· Do you expect what would appear when you pressed the Esc key?

Menu

· What would you do when you went back to battle from the menu?
· Where should you proceed with control the volume?
· Do you know the difference between Quit game and Back to Main?

Resolution

The game was organized in stages, not in real-time to give a detailed explanation. This makes it easier to provide information about items or during the healing time. This allows users to feel like they are growing up by defeating enemies in each level.
