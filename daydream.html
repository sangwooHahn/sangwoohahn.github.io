Game Design / UI · UX Design

Daydream

Daydream is made with a short-term game development project that was created through 3 months of collaboration with one programmer. It is a form of action game based on medieval fantasy.

Target Users

Daydream is a game for people who like to enjoy games and feel a sense of accomplishment through difficult cleaning. It is set in medieval Western fantasy and contains its own story and worldview. It is designed for heavy game lovers such as stories and settings in this background.

Game Graphics

Character Design

Considering the limited development time, the character expressed movement with frame-by-frame animation using a 2D sprite. The character has a simple appearance that is designed not to take much time to produce various movements. However, it shows a different charm by adding cruel descriptions to cute graphics. In order to preserve the identity of the character, each character is assigned a symbolic color and weapon.

Character Motions

Boss Design

Boss "Golem of Knowledge" was designed in the form of a golem consisting of bookshelves. Boss design with a 2D sprite make into parts to facilitate movement. Hands that are not directly connected to the shoulder are designed to be advantageous in attacking enemies.

Environment Design

To avoid the monotony of 2D characters’ movement that only moves left and right, the background designed in 3D. It shows the depth inside of the tower. The background of each floor is made up of objects related to the monsters that meet on that floor.

Game Interface

Interface Research

Daydream is a game designed for PC and console games. To satisfy all of this, the user interface is designed not to use a mouse. Interfaces are important even during the game. After one battle, the player needs time to acquire items and healing. In order to effectively show various items and healing information, it replaced with an interface, not character manipulation.

Usability Tests

Title & Character Page

· Do you understand how to operate it?
· Do you realize right away that you have to choose two characters to proceed?
· Is the icon enough to mark the selected character?

During the Game

· Do you realize which is a user's health point and how much it has?
· Do you know the key to switching characters?
· Where do you look to find out the health point of your fellow characters?
· Were you able to find your boss' health point?
· If you forgot control keys, where would you find them?
· Where would you like to see a detailed description of the skill?
· Do you know that each skills consumes a different amount of energy?
· Do you expect what would appear when you pressed the Esc key?

Menu

· What would you do when you went back to battle from the menu?
· Where should you proceed with control the volume?
· Do you know the difference between Quit game and Back to Main?
